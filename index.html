Who We Are

Empowering Muslim students to excel, lead, and make a lasting impact.

Our Story

IHSAAN was founded with a simple but powerful belief: that every Muslim student deserves equal access to the mentorship, networks, and opportunities needed to build a meaningful career. The word Ihsaan means excellence — striving for the highest standard in everything we do, as a form of devotion to God.

We work across universities in the UK and Ireland to bridge the gap between talent and opportunity for Muslim students who are too often overlooked.

Our Mission

To empower every Muslim student in the UK and Ireland with the mentorship, resources, and professional networks they need to secure their dream careers — and through those careers, contribute to a more ethical and God-conscious society.

Our Vision

A future where no Muslim student is held back by a lack of access or representation. We envision a generation of Muslim professionals who lead with integrity, serve their communities, and shape industries for the better.

Why It Matters

Research consistently shows that young Muslims face significant barriers in education and employment. The Social Mobility Commission found that Muslim graduates are less likely to secure professional-level jobs than their peers, even with the same qualifications. IHSAAN exists to tackle these disparities head-on — providing students with the confidence, connections, and career tools they need to succeed.

The Prophetic Standard

"Verily, God has prescribed Ihsaan (excellence) in everything."
— Hadith 17, An-Nawawi's Forty

This prophetic tradition is the foundation of everything we do. Excellence is not just a professional aspiration — it is a spiritual obligation. Whether mentoring a student, organising a conference, or building a new programme, we hold ourselves to the highest standard because our faith demands it.

Career Conferences

Connecting Muslim students with leading employers, role models, and career pathways.

What Are Career Conferences?

Our career conferences are IHSAAN's flagship programme — large-scale, professionally organised events that bring Muslim students face-to-face with employers, industry leaders, and successful professionals. Held at universities and venues across the UK and Ireland, these conferences give students the chance to explore career paths, attend skills workshops, and build the networks that will shape their futures.

Faithful Talent

Connecting Muslim students directly with employers who value faith-driven talent.

The Challenge

Muslim graduates in the UK face a persistent employment gap. Despite having the same qualifications as their peers, they are significantly less likely to secure professional-level roles. The Social Mobility Commission found that young Muslims are "excluded, discriminated against, or failed" throughout their journey into employment.

The Opportunity

Employers across every sector want to build diverse, high-performing teams — but many lack a direct connection to Muslim talent. At the same time, Muslim students often lack the networks and access points that open doors to competitive roles. Faithful Talent bridges this gap by creating structured pathways between students and employers.

How We Work

Faithful Talent operates through three core pillars:

  • Access — Our career conferences bring students face-to-face with employers across finance, law, tech, consulting, and more. Attendees gain direct exposure to roles and hiring managers.
  • Mentorship & Readiness — Through My Muslim Mentor, career guides, and the Opportunities Tracker, students develop the skills and knowledge to compete at the highest level.
  • Connection — Faithful Talent employer events create intimate, high-quality settings where shortlisted students meet directly with hiring teams — moving beyond CVs to genuine conversations.

Our Impact

Faithful Talent has already delivered real results for students and employers alike:

  • Deutsche Bank Event — 50 students attended a tailored networking and recruitment evening, with multiple students progressing to interview stages.
  • Tijara Ventures — 5 internship offers extended from a single Faithful Talent event.

What Are Haqqathons?

Haqqathons bring together the brightest value-driven young people for an intensive weekend of building — creating impactful technology solutions to real-world problems. Part hackathon, part social enterprise incubator, these events are where ideas become action.

Our mission: Empowering principled visionaries to solve real problems for real impact.

Our vision: A world guided by ethics and values, where integrity shapes decisions, compassion drives actions, and stewardship sustains progress.

Career Guides

Practical, step-by-step resources to help you land the role you want.

What Are Career Guides?

Our Career Guides are free, in-depth resources written specifically for Muslim students navigating the UK job market. Whether you are writing your first CV, preparing for an assessment centre, or deciding between career paths, these guides give you the practical knowledge and confidence to take the next step.

Topics Covered

  • Networking — building professional relationships authentically
  • Industry overviews — breaking into finance, law, tech, consulting, and the public sector
